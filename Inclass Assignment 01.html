Human Brain

Introduction to Neuroscience

Neuroscience is the study of the structure and function of the brain and nervous system.

Subfields of Neuroscience:

Subfield Description
Cognitive Neuroscience The study of the neural mechanisms underlying cognition.
Neuroanatomy The study of the structure of the brain and nervous system.
Neurophysiology The study of the functions of the brain and nervous system.

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity refers to the brain's ability to change and adapt throughout life.

Types of Neuroplasticity:

Type Description
Synaptic Plasticity The strengthening or weakening of connections between neurons.
Neuronal Plasticity The birth or death of new neurons.
Functional Plasticity Changes in the way neurons communicate with each other.

Mental Health

Mental health refers to our emotional, psychological, and social well-being.

Common Mental Health Conditions:

Condition Description
Depression A mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness.
Anxiety A mental health condition characterized by feelings of worry or fear.
Bipolar Disorder A mood disorder characterized by extreme mood swings.

Key Brain Functions

The brain is responsible for controlling various functions, including movement, sensation, perception, and cognition.

Brain Regions and Their Functions:

Region Function
Cerebrum Controls movement, sensation, perception, and cognition.
Cerebellum Coordinates muscle movements and maintains posture.
Brainstem Regulates basic functions such as breathing, heart rate, and blood pressure.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the human brain is a complex and fascinating organ that is still not fully understood.
